GRP Biathlon Wrap: Jan 10 Weekend

Last Updated: 12.Jan.2015

Five GRP biathletes resumed competition over the weekend after a holiday hiatus. In a rare moment of converging schedules, they all flew to Munich, Germany last Tuesday en route to three different competitions.

National Team members Susan Dunklee and Hannah Dreissigacker traveled to the biathlon-crazy city of Oberhof, Germany for World Cup 4. On a blustery Friday afternoon that saw many of the top competitors taking uncharacteristic turns in the penalty loop, both Dunklee and Dreissigacker scored World Cup points in the 7.5k sprint by placing inside the top 40. Dunklee led the US women in 21st place with thee penalties, and Hannah was next for the team in 37th with two penalties. Only two women in the 93-person field shot clean for the race. Dunklee was the lone American woman in Sunday's 30-person mass start competition, and though she struggled on the range with 8 misses, she skied the 13th-fastest course time and worked her way up from the back into 27th place.

Meanwhile, Clare Egan made her debut on the IBU Cup in Duszniki, Poland. Egan is one of three American women biathletes there who are competing for a start spot at World Cup 6 in Antholz, Italy. Unfortunately, she came down with a cold while traveling to Europe, and had to sit out of Friday's race, and then had a tough day on course as well as on the range on Saturday in the sprint. She was the third American in 77th place with 5 misses. Egan competes next weekend in IBU Cup 5, which has been relocated to Ridnaun/Ridanna, Italy.

Michael Gibson and Ethan Dreissigacker are taking part in the second annual Alpen Cup club trip, which is for top American biathletes interested in gaining European racing experience. And for the second year in a row, they are having terrible luck with weather! The races scheduled this weekend in Langdorf, Germany were cancelled last-minute due to lack of snow, leaving the biathletes with no choice but to jog around in the Bavarian Forest and feast on Schnitzel. The Alpen Cup schedule for next weekend is also up in the air, but the athletes and coaches remain optimistic that they will be able to find a valuable competition experience somewhere.

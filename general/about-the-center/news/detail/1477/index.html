Nature Activities with the Craftsbury Outdoor Center

Last Updated: 28.Apr.2015

This summer the Outdoor Center will be offering opportunities to learn about local flora and fauna and help with ecosystem-monitoring projects. All are welcome and no prior experience is necessary.

Wildflower walks kick off Saturday, May 30
Learn to identify local wildflowers, shrubs, and trees, and hear about their habitat preferences, unique life histories and roles in local “natural communities” on guided wildflower walks this summer. The first of these three will walks is the last Saturday in May, 10:00am-12:30pm, meeting on the Activity Center porch.

Join plant ecologist Eva Dannenberg to explore the wild plants in the woods and cedar swamp surrounding the Craftsbury Outdoor Center. In years past we have seen more than 30 different wildflowers, ferns, and shrubs. Leisurely paced, on- and off-trail walking. Bring water, a hand lens, and a field guide to your favorite group of plants if you have one. Eva will also be offering 2 more wild plant walks at the Outdoor Center this season, one in July and one in September, dates TBD. For more information, email Eva at saxifraga21@gmail.com or call 802-586-7737.

Wildflower monitoring
One of the Outdoor Center’s new projects focuses on wildflower monitoring. We will meet three times throughout the growing season to collect data as a group. The aim of this project is to gather long-term data on the Center’s wildflower community by recording which species are present in a variety of habitats and estimating species abundance. We will also monitor some aspects of plant phenology. Phenology is the study of natural biological life cycle events (ex: bud burst, flower opening, or leaf fall) that are influenced by both habitat and climate conditions. Phenology is important as some of the impacts of climate change on ecosystems can be explored through long-term phenological event monitoring. In addition to providing the Center and its visitors with information, this project will contribute to the broader scientific community, as the data we collect will be entered into the National Phenology Network Nature’s Notebook database. Nature’s Notebook is a program where both amateur and professional naturalists record phenological plant and animal observations to create long-term data sets for scientific discovery and decision-making.

Biodiversity Survey
Another new project starting this spring is the Center’s biodiversity survey. The goal of this project is to record the flora and fauna present on the Outdoor Center’s property, the surrounding trail system, and the Hosmer Ponds. Data for this project data can be collected at any time. If you are interested in getting involved and have a smartphone, you can create an iNaturalist profile, download the iNaturalist app (Android or iPhone), and bring your phone with you on a walk, run, ski, or snowshoe (you can record your location, photos and observations and then sync them when you're within data range again - wifi or cell signal). No smartphone? You can use a camera instead and upload your pics to the website. Take a photo of the species you observe, note the location (ex: the trail you’re on, the number of the nearest intersection, Great or Little Hosmer Pond), and upload your photos and observations to the Craftsbury Outdoor Center Biodiversity project page on the iNaturalist website. For more information please visit the project’s web page.

Whether you are a beginner or experienced naturalist (or somewhere in between), we encourage you to get involved with the Center’s nature activities. If you have any questions, please contact Kaitlynn Miller at kaitlynnbmiller@gmail.com. Watch for updates here on the website, or send a message to Kaitlynn asking to be added to the mailing list and we’ll let you know when the dates are set.

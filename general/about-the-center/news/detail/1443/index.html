Patterson 5th in American Birkebeiner, Sargent races at World Championships

Last Updated: 25.Feb.2015

It was another jam packed race weekend for the Green Racing Project skiers as they near the end of the 2014-2015 season. Over in Falun, Sweden, Ida Sargent contested her first race of World Championships, while the stateside GRP members took on the American Birkebeiner and the Stowe Derby.

Four GRP skiers, two men and two women, made the long trip to Hayward, Wisconsin to race the 51k Birkie. Usually the marathon event is preceded by Elite Sprints on Main Street in Hayward, but this year plummeting temperatures forced the organizers to cancel the sprints and all focus was on the main event. The women’s elite wave was the first to start at 8 AM, and included GRP skiers Caitlin Patterson and Kaitlynn Miller. Although American Holly Brooks took the women’s win in Hayward, Caitlin P. skied a smart race to take 5th. She lost contact with the 4 person lead women’s pack early on, but then worked with the skiers behind her and eventually pulled away when a train of men caught her. Kaitlynn Miller was the 18th woman overall in her Birkie Debut. On the men’s side, Andrew Dougherty and Gordon Vermeer were part of a bunch sprint on Main St. and ended up 21st and 23rd overall, respectively. Also in the men’s race were GRP alums Bryan Cook and Matt Briggs, who had impressive finishes in 14th and 24th overall. Former GRP skier Maria Stuber also took 25th in the women’s race and 3rd in her age group. Impressive for a full time coach! In the battle of the GRP rowers, lightweight Phil Henson came out on top, but Steve Whelpley was only 39 seconds behind in his second Birkie. Rower Maggie Fellows had an awesome debut as well, taking 78th for women and 14th in her age group.

Closer to home in Stowe, VT, three GRP skiers attempted to become “Derby Meister” at the Stowe Derby by having the fastest combined skate and classic time from racing the 20k, mostly downhill, course two times in quick succession. Pete Hegman came the closest by taking 10th in the skate leg and 2nd in the classic, good for 3rd place in the Derby Meister. Jake Barton was 3rd in skate and 7th in classic for the 5th best overall time, and Alex Schulz was 23rd in skate and 6th in classic for 10th overall.

Finally, Ida Sargent raced the classic sprint at World Championships in Falun, and qualified 29th. She then faced a tough quarterfinal heat and took 6th place to remain in 29th. The top US result on the day was Vermonter Sophie Caldwell’s 10th place. However, just a few days later in the 10k skate individual, US skiers Jessie Diggins and Caitlin Gregg made history with the first podiums ever for US women in a distance race at World Championships. Diggins was 2nd behind Sweden’s Charlotte Kalla, and Gregg took 3rd. This unprecedented result bodes well for the future of US women’s skiing, and is an incredible accomplishment for both skiers, but especially for Caitlin Gregg racing in her third World Championships at the age of 34.
Next up for the GRP skiers: Caitlin Patterson and Liz Guiney are racing OPA Cups in Rogla, Slovenia this weekend, and then Caitlin will join Ida on the World Cup for races in Lahti, Finland and Drammen and Oslo, Norway.

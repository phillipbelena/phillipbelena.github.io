Dunklee and Dreissigacker cap a successful World Cup season in Oslo, Norway

Last Updated: 23.Mar.2014

For fans of GRP biathlete Susan Dunklee it was worth the wait to see her notch her first ever World Cup podium this weekend in Oslo, Norway in the final World Cup sprint of the season. But it was just one great result in a string of strong performances put forth by her and her US biathlon teammates this season. Susan and fellow GRP biathlete Hannah Dreissigacker have both had personal best results this year in World Cup and Olympic competition, with Susan taking a string of top ten’s and Hannah breaking into the points on several occasions. This weekend’s competitions in Oslo were a chance for them to finish the season with a bang, and a chance for Susan to achieve a new personal best.

Susan has had some of her best results in sprint races, and a 4th in Antholz earlier this year showed her how tantalizingly close the podium could be. Last Thursday in Oslo, she showed everybody that she was ready to take that step, and was on the other side of the “podium cusp”. After cleaning both shooting stages she crossed the line in 3rd, but then had to wait for all of the other finishers to cross the line before she knew her spot was secure. In the end, only Darya Domracheva from Belarus and Tora Berger from Norway were faster, and Susan climbed onto the steps of her first ever World Cup podium. It was the first podium for the US women in biathlon in over 20 years and a big stepping stone in Susan’s career. Hannah, who started her sprint just as Susan was finishing, was fueled by motivation from her teammate’s success.  However, she was not quite able to capitalize and finish high enough to make the pursuit the following day. She struggled on the range with 5 misses and finished 75th in her last World Cup race of the season.

Starting in 3rd in Saturday’s pursuit, Susan was where she needed to be to notch another top finish. In the first loop she was holding the gap to leader Domracheva, but took a wrong line on a tricky downhill corner and found herself on the ground. She noticed that several clips of ammunition fell out of her rifle during the crash, so she made a quick decision to pick them up and hopefully save some time on the range. However, her chosen place to store them, down her spandex tights, didn’t pan out when the clips slid down to her ankles. She ended up still needing to get clips from the range officials, and the lost time from the ammunition debacle plus the crash took her out of contention for the lead. She kept her head and shot well though, only missing one shot in her first stage and one in her fourth stage. Coming into the final ski lap she was still within reach of the podium in fourth, but lost a little bit of juice and was ultimately passed by two of the best skiers on the biathlon World Cup, Domracheva and Kaisa Makarainen of Finland. However, 6th place is still her 3rd best result ever, so it was another good day in the books for Susan.

Sunday’s mass start was the last race of the biathlon season, and for Susan her first mass start since the Olympics. Unfortunately, her shooting wasn’t quite on par with usual, and she finished her four stages with 6 misses total and 18th place overall. A particularly frustrating third stage saddled her with three penalty laps, but she was able to regroup, clean her last stage, and finish hard to end her season on a strong note. Susan has plenty of motivation for summer training now, and we’re very certain that her podium appearance from Thursday will just be the first of many amazing finishes to come.

Congrats to both Susan and Hannah for a fantastic World Cup and Olympic season, and we hope they take some well-deserved rest before gearing up for next year. 

As always, results from the weekend are available on the IBU Datacenter.

Sargent races post-Olympic World Cup in Lahti, Finland

Last Updated: 06.Mar.2014

The Olympics may be over, but the racing on the cross country World Cup is just heating up. The World Cup field flew from Sochi to Scandinavia, where they will finish out their race season in Finland, Sweden, and Norway. This past weekend saw double skate races in Lahti, Finland; a skate sprint on Saturday and a 10k skate on Sunday. Some were out for redemption after disappointing Olympic races, while others were looking to defend overall or sprint titles.

The GRP’s Ida Sargent qualified for the sprint heats at the Olympics just over two weeks ago, but in Lahti the competition for heats was fierce and Ida ended up on the outside of qualifying in 44th. However, she was less than 2 seconds out of 30th place and many strong World Cup sprinters found themselves out of qualifying as well. Ida tends to be stronger in classic than skate, so she’ll be looking forward to the remaining two classic sprints.

The highlight of the day for the Americans was the double podium of Kikkan Randall and Sophie Caldwell, a Peru, VT native. Kikkan and Sophie were the only American women to qualify for the heats, with Sadie Bjornsen and Jessie Diggins out with colds. After taking 11th and 25th, respectively, in qualifying, Kikkan and Sophie kept moving up through their heats to make the final. The course in Lahti was soft and sugary, with deep ruts appearing on some parts of the course by the end of the day. Kikkan was able to surge ahead and take the victory, a bittersweet one for her after she failed to advance out of her Olympic quarterfinal, where she was favored to win gold. Sophie was the surprise podium of the day in 3rd place. Even though she finished 6th in the Olympic sprint, she is a relative rookie to the World Cup. Saturday was the first time two American women have made a World Cup podium, and only the second time any Americans have accomplished the feat, following Bill Koch and Tim Caldwell, Sophie’s uncle, back in the 1970’s. After the victory, Kikkan has a lead in the Crystal Globe competition for World Cup sprints, and will be looking to hang on through the last two classic sprints.

After the exciting sprint day, Sunday’s 10k skate had fewer fireworks for the Americans. Ida didn’t start the 10k, and the top American was Kikkan in 21st. The World Cup just keeps right on going, with the Drammen classic city sprint on Wednesday the 5th of March. This weekend the World Cup field will take on the famous Holmenkollen 30k and 50k in Oslo, Norway before heading to World Cup finals in Falun, Sweden. Stay tuned for results and race reports!

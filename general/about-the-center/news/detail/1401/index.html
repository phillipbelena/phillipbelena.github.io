GRP Skiers Race at US Senior Nationals in Houghton, Michigan

Last Updated: 13.Jan.2015

The GRP skiers were back at it after a short holiday break! The most recent races found them in Houghton, Michigan, way up on Michigan’s Upper Peninsula for US Nationals. The four day race series is used as selection for European trips later in the season such as Under23 World Championships, OPA Cups, and World Championships.

The first day, January 4th, was a 10k/15k individual start freestyle race with cold and snowy conditions (a theme for the entire week). The men raced 2x a 7.5k loop. The top GRP men’s result was Gordon Vermeer in 18th, and he was followed by Alex Howe in 34th, Pete Hegman in 58th, Andrew Dougherty in 71st, and Jake Barton in 85th. In the women’s race, and Caitlin Patterson was just off the podium in 4th while Liz Guiney took 18th.

Organizers delayed the classic sprint due to cold temperatures, but it was able to go off without a hitch on the 6th of January, one day later than planned. Four GRP skiers made the heats, with Gordon going through in 11th and Alex H. in 23rd. In the women’s race, Liz took the 4th qualifying spot and Kaitlynn Miller snuck through in 30th. Gordon and Alex both took 4th in their respective heats and didn’t advance from the quarterfinals, ending up 16th and 20th overall. Kaitlynn also took 4th in her heat and moved up to 20th overall, while Liz was able to improve on her qualifying spot and finish 3rd, for her first podium at Senior Nationals.

The 20k/30k classic day brought with it more cold weather, but a surprising break in the snowfall that allowed for solid tracks and relatively fast conditions. The men took it out first in the 30k, with 3x a 10k loop. Andrew and Gordon stayed in the chase pack and finished 17th and 20th, respectively. Alex H. and Pete also skied much of the race together and finished in 30th and 32nd, while Alex Schulz and Jake Barton took 55th and 66th. In the women’s race, the 3 GRP ladies stayed up near the front for most of the first lap, and ultimately took 2nd, 6th, and 13th. Caitlin’s 2nd place was a personal best at Nationals, as was Kaitlynn’s 13th, and Liz’s 6th was her best in a distance race.

In the final race, a freestyle sprint, the GRP had 6 skiers make the rounds this time, with Alex H. in 10th, Gordon in 20th, and Alex S. in 27th. On the women’s side, Caitlin posted a strong qualifier in 4th, Liz followed in 17th, and Kaitlynn was 27th. Alex H. and Gordon ended up in the same heat and their 4th and 5th place finishes in that heat weren’t enough to advance, neither was the other Alex’s 6th in his heat. Caitlin, Liz, and Kaitlynn had the unlucky draw of all contesting the same quarterfinal heat, which made it difficult for all three to advance. Caitlin took 2nd and an automatic semifinal spot, while Liz’s 3rd was enough to gain her a lucky loser spot. Kaitlynn was 5th in the heat, which moved her up in the overall to 25th. Neither Caitlin nor Liz advanced out of the semifinals, and Caitlin ended up 8th while Liz took 10th overall.

The skiers are now back in Craftsbury for a while to recover and train before the Craftsbury SuperTours in late January. In the meantime, they’ll race the Rumford Eastern Cup and potentially the UVM Carnival. Stay tuned for updates!

US Nationals Results:
10k/15k Freestyle Individual
Classic Sprint
20k/30k Classic Mass Start
Freestyle Sprint

Susan Dunklee battled a blizzard in Saturday's Sprint at 2015 Biathlon World Championships

GRP at Biathlon World Championships

Last Updated: 09.Mar.2015

Last weekend marked the opening of the 2015 Biathlon World Championships in Kontiolahti, Finland. Among the athletes representing the US there are three GRP women, Susan Dunklee, Hannah Dreissigacker, and Clare Egan.

Racing kicked off last Thursday with the Mixed Relay. Susan and Hannah were joined by teammates Lowell Bailey and Lief Nordren, in that order. Susan continued her streak of strong relay starts, skiing the second-fastest course time for the first leg, and using only one spare round in each stage to tag off to Hannah in third place. Unfortunately, Hannah suffered a bad prone stage and incurred one penalty lap, but she hit all of her standing targets using two spares, and skied fast enough to tag Lowell in 13th place out of 26 teams. Lowell had a perfect leg, shooting lean in both stages, which moved the team up to 11th place. Competing with a tight group of teams all vying for a place in the top 10, Leif Nordgren stayed calm and used only one spare in prone and then shot clean in standing, bringing team USA to a solid 8th place finish.

Saturday's Sprint events were characterized by gusty winds, and in the women's race, a blizzard to boot. Team USA got off to a good start with all four American men overcoming unpredictable winds to finish inside the top 60 to qualify for Sunday's pursuit. About an hour before the women's race, a the winds joined forces with a blizzard that left a layer of fresh powder on the trail for the early starters to plow through. Susan Dunklee, wearing bib 15, was one of those early starters. Faced with the task of breaking trail for those behind her, Susan, who often skis a top-10 ski time and is no stranger to the top 5, notched the 20th fastest time of the day. She also encountered some of the worst winds of the afternoon, and though she managed to miss only three total targets, she clocked the 103rd shooting time of the day, out of 103 finishers. Currently ranked 16th on the overall World Cup standings, Susan had to settle for 42nd. But she reacted to her struggle with grace: "I averaged over a minute per stage waiting out some big gusts. It must be my new record for the longest time I have spent at the range during a race. Considering my struggles, I am very grateful to have a start in tomorrow's pursuit."

As the snow began to wane, bib number 56 Hannah Dreissigacker was skiing and shooting fast, clocking the 25th-fasted ski time and 26th shooting time. But the wind got the best of her, and four misses in prone and two in standing landed Hannah right outside of the pursuit qualification, in 61st place. Annelies Cook followed Hannah in 68th place with five misses. 

Finally, Clare Egan took to the course wearing bib 70. With fast tracks and skis, Clare managed a 39th ski time, which combined with a shooting time also in the 30's and four total misses, was good enough for 40th place. This was Clare's first pursuit qualification and also marked her first World Cup point. "This was an 'anything can happen' kind of day," said Clare of her new personal best result. 

In Sunday's pursuit, Susan started 5 seconds behind Clare and passed her after about half a kilometer, on her way to the 3rd-fastest ski time of the day. Despite six misses (1,1,2,2,) Susan managed to move up in the pursuit to finish 34th. Clare had seven misses (1,1,3,2) and moved back to 52nd. On the men's side, Tim Burke had the top result for the US in 20th place. 

Racing continues on Wednesday with the Individual. At the 2012 World Championships in Ruhpolding, Germany, Susan was 5th in this event.

Summer Ski Programs

In addition to our popular summer BKL camp and junior ski camps, we offer dryland training programs for youth skiers ages 8 and up. Our sessions for younger kids focus primarily on being active, having fun, and working on fundamentals. As skiers move up through our programs, they embark on more focused training, including rollerskiing, running, strength, and more. Programs are generally organized by age, but final selection/placement will depend on participants' needs and coaches' discretion. Not sure where you might fit in? Contact our coaching staff. Want to participate but worried about affording the program fee? Apply for a scholarship here. Don't forget that Craftsbury Outdoor Center Membership is a prerequisite for registering in our summer training programs.

BKL Development

Our BKL Development program is meant for kids ages 8-12 who are enthusiastic about being active and outside. The program costs $200 and meets twice per week, starting in June and running into October. In the summer, practices will be Tuesdays and Thursdays from 4:00pm to 5:15pm. When school starts again in the fall, practices will switch to Wednesdays and Fridays at 4:00pm. This program is led by various members of our coaching staff with help from GRP skiers as well. Activities will include running, hill bounding, rollerskiing, biking, strength, and canoeing, with a healthy mix of fun and games! 

Participants are required to have the following: current Craftsbury Outdoor Center Membership; running shoes and active clothing; and ideally, a mountain bike and helmet (though not having those should not discourage registration as we have a limited number of bikes for use--contact Anna if you anticipate needing to rent a bike). Rollerskis are not required: they are used infrequently by this group and are provided by the Outdoor Center.

BKL Competitive

Our BKL competitive program is aimed at kids ages 9-14 who have significant skiing experience, enjoy racing, and are very excited about improving their skills. The program costs $300 and runs from May into November. Prior to the end of the school year, this group will meet twice per week: Wednesday at 4:00pm and Sundays at 9:00am. Starting the week of June 16th, it will meet three times per week: 4:00pm on Tuesdays, and 9:00am on Fridays and Sundays. When school starts again in the fall, practices will be Tuesdays and Thursdays at 4:00pm and 9:00am on Sundays. Training includes rollerskiing, pole-running, running, hill bounding, strength training, biathlon and other activities. The training program will be designed and led by coaches Anna Schulz, Audrey Mangan, Jake Barton, and Ollie Burruss, with occasional coaching from biathlon coach Sam Dougherty, GRP coach Pepa Miloucheva, and GRP members as well.

Participants are required to have the following: current Craftsbury Outdoor Center Membership; running shoes and active clothing; a bike helmet; at least one high-visibility shirt or vest; a mountain bike (though not having a bike should not discourage registration as we have a limited number of bikes for rent); and ideally, skate rollerskis, boots, and poles with rollerski ferrules (again, we do have a limited supply of rollerskis, boots, and poles that will be available for rent).

High School Competitive

Our High School Competitive program was introduced in 2018 and is aimed at kids roughly ages 13-16 who are excited about training and racing but are relatively new to it or are primarily concerned with high school racing. The program will cost $200 and will meet 2-3 times per week: Fridays at 4:00pm and Sundays at 9:00am prior to the end of school, and then Wednesdays and Thursdays at 9:00am during the summer. When school starts again in the fall, practices will be at 9:00am on Sundays only. Some practices will be run in conjunction with the EC Racing practices. Participants will be encouraged to join in the summer Tuesday night running and biking race series as well. This program is led by coaches Anna Schulz, Audrey Mangan, Jake Barton, and Ollie Burruss with help from GRP members. 

Participants are required to have the following: current Craftsbury Outdoor Center Membership; running shoes and active clothing; and ideally, a mountain bike and helmet (though not having those should not discourage registration as we have bikes available for rent--contact us if you anticipate needing to rent a bike). Eastern Cup Racing

This program is designed for experienced junior racers ages 14-20. Many current program participants are successful junior skiers at the state, regional, and national levels. The program costs $400 for the summer/fall session. Practices for the summer session will start on Wednesday, May 1st. While school is still in session, practices will be Wednesdays and Fridays at 4:00pm and Sundays at 9:00am. During the summer, practices will be Tuesdays through Fridays at 9:00am and Sundays at 9:00am as well. Saturdays will be a "flex" day that will include practices/training sessions as needed. Participants are expected to attend practices at least 3 days per week, follow a training plan on days not at Craftsbury, and keep a training log. Participation in summer training is a prerequisite for those interested in joining our winter program. Interested skiers who have not skied with Craftsbury before should contact coach Anna as soon as possible prior to registering: anna.schulz@craftsbury.com. New participants will not be accepted after July 1st. When school starts again in the fall, practices will be Tuesdays - Fridays at 4:00pm and Sundays at 9:00am. Training will include rollerskiing, pole-running, running, hill bounding, strength training, biathlon and other activities. The training program will be designed and led by our full-time coaching staff. 

Participants are required to have the following: current Craftsbury Outdoor Center Membership; running shoes and active clothing; a bike helmet; a camelbak or some way to comfortably carry water/food while running; at least one high-visibility shirt or vest; a mountain bike; skate and classic rollerskis, boots, and skate and classic poles with rollerski ferrules.

College Training

Our college summer training program is for both current and rising college students who ski competitively for their schools. This is a small, experienced, and dedicated group that works with both the junior program and coaches and occasionally with the GRP and Pepa as well. Training sessions are generally Tuesdays through Fridays at 9:00am and Sundays at 9:00am as well, though additional sessions may be added depending on weekly training demands. Participants in this group are welcome to begin training with the team as early in the summer as academic schedules permit. Training programs are designed collaboratively by junior and GRP coaches, with flexibility possible to accommodate individual and college team plans as well. Enrollment in this group is by prior arrangement only. Contact coach Anna with any questions: anna.schulz@craftsbury.com.

Biathlon

While we occasionally incorporate biathlon into our regular programming for kids ages 8+, we also offer additional biathlon practices for those wishing to hone their skills. Our extra biathlon sessions are currently only for skiers already enrolled in our BKL competitive, High School Competitive, or EC racing programs. Our biathlon sessions are run by Ethan Dreissigacker and Sam Dougherty, our experienced biathlon coaches. Summer sessions will start on Sunday, June 16th. Practices will be Tuesdays at 5:00pm (group 2--our smaller/younger biathletes), Fridays at 11:00am (group 1--our taller/older biathletes), and Sundays at 11:00am (groups 1 & 2). Skiers wishing to join this program who do NOT have prior biathlon experience should contact the coaching staff.

Green Racing Project

This training group is our elite training program primarily for individuals who are pursuing post-collegiate racing at the highest levels. More information is available on the GRP website. 

Part of our mission is to make lifelong sport accessible to one and all. As such, we have a scholarship program to support kids who may not otherwise be able to participate in our programs. We also offer assistance to families in our programs whose children have qualified for and are traveling to major skiing competitions. You can complete a scholarship application here. Feel free to contact junior coach Anna Schulz (anna.schulz@craftsbury.com) with questions about scholarships.

Questions? Not sure which group to sign up for? Contact us via skiing@craftsbury.com for more details on any program.
